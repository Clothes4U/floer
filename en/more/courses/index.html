Courses

The following courses are foundational courses in Floer homology and related fields, suitable for students who wish to enter this field. Undergraduate mathematics foundation courses can refer to Harvard University’s Math 55A (Honors Algebra) and Math 55B (Honors Analysis). Additional courses that may be helpful include basic topology, homological algebra, functional analysis, partial differential equations, elliptic PDE, Lie groups and Lie algebras, group representation theory, etc.

Differential Geometry

Course Page | Differential geometry is a branch of mathematics that studies geometric structures on manifolds and is fundamental to understanding Floer homology. This course introduces basic concepts such as manifolds, tangent bundles, Riemannian metrics, and curvature, laying the foundation for subsequent study of topology and geometry.

Introductory References (Peking University course: Differentiable Manifolds):

  1. Introduction to Differentiable Manifolds (in Chinese), Weihuan Chen
  2. Lectures on Differential Geometry (in Chinese), Shing-Shen Chern, Weihuan Chen
  3. Differential Geometry: Bundles, Connections, Metrics and Curvature, Chapters 1-16, Clifford H. Taubes
  4. Differential Forms in Algebraic Topology, Raoul Bott, Loring W. Tu
Algebraic Topology

Course Page | Algebraic topology studies the properties of topological spaces through algebraic methods and is an important theoretical foundation for Floer homology. This course introduces basic concepts such as homology groups and cohomology, as well as important computational tools such as CW complexes, Mayer-Vietoris sequences, and universal coefficient theorems.

Introductory References (Peking University course: Homology Theory):

  1. Homology Theory (in Chinese), Boju Jiang
  2. Lectures on Algebraic Topology, Chapters 1-3, Haynes Miller
  3. Algebraic Topology, Chapters 2-3, Allen Hatcher
  4. An Introduction to Homological Algebra, Charles A. Weibel

The following are advanced courses that can be studied as needed.

Floer Homology Overview

Course Page | Floer homology is a generalization of Morse homology to infinite-dimensional spaces and is an important tool in low-dimensional topology and symplectic geometry. This course introduces the basic theory of sutured 3-manifolds and Floer homology, including Heegaard Floer homology, monopole Floer homology, instanton Floer homology, and more.

Knots, SU(2), and the Pillowcase

Course Page | This short course introduces the relationship between knots, SU(2) representations, and the pillowcase, focusing on SU(2) non-abelian representations of 3-manifolds and applications of instanton Floer homology. The course starts from a problem in Kirby’s problem list, understanding the properties of Dehn surgery manifolds by studying submanifolds in the SU(2) character variety of the boundary torus of knot complements (homeomorphic to the pillowcase).
