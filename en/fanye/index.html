About

I am an assistant professor at Peking University.

Previously, I was a Benjamin Peirce Fellow and a Simons collaboration postdoc at the Harvard Math Department, mentored by Peter Kronheimer.

I completed my Ph.D. in Mathematics at University of Cambridge under the supervision of Jacob Rasmussen.

Research Interests

I am interested in low-dimensional topology, especially knot theory, gauge theory, and Floer homology. Related topics include:

  • Heegaard Floer, Seiberg-Witten Floer, instanton Floer, symplectic Floer homology
  • Embedded contact homology, Khovanov homology, Khovanov–Rozansky homology
  • Contact topology, quantum topology, geometric analysis, mathematical physics
