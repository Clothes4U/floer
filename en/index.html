Welcome to Floer Homology Team!

Floer Homology Team was established by Fan Ye in 2026 at the School of Mathematical Sciences, Peking University. The team’s main research directions include: 3-manifold Floer homology, symplectic Floer homology, and quantum link homology. Floer homology is a generalization of Morse homology to infinite-dimensional settings and serves as an important tool in low-dimensional topology and symplectic geometry, with wide applications in mathematical physics, contact topology, quantum topology, and related fields.

The team currently consists of 2 PhD students and 2 undergraduate students. For more detailed information, please visit the People page. Introductions and introductory materials for each research direction can be found on the Research page, published results are available on the Publications page, and news, courses, and more are provided on the More page.

If you are interested in our research directions or have any questions, please visit the Join page to learn how to join the team, or contact Fan Ye directly at flyye@math.pku.edu.cn.
